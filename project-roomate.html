Integrating user insights into the design of a roommate-seeking app

A case study for how the user-centered design process helps roommate-seeking platform built

What is RooMate?

RooMate is the Capstone project I did independently while studying in the Thinkful/Bloc Design Program. User-centered design mindset is applied throughout the design process from user research to A/B preference testing to usability testing on a hi-fi interactive prototype. It takes 3 months to complete the project.

At the background of a highly expanding urban population and late marriage age, RooMate app is aiming to help people, especially millennials living in cities, find ideal roommates.

Problem

There are a few trends of note: 54% of the worldâ€™s population live in urban communities, the average marriage age for men is 29 (up from 26 two decades ago) and for women is 27 (up from 23 in the same time period). Given these trends, city dwellers tend to spend most of their twenties living with roommates. Finding and keeping a good roommate, however, gets harder as more and more people live in cities.

Problem Breakdown

- How to connect and match potential roommates?
- How to make people feel secure using a product to help seek roommates?
- How can people view detailed information of potential roommates without worrying privacy issues?

Outcomes and Success Metrics

- Design an interactive prototype at iOS platform with basic functions addressing the problem statement.
- Perform usability testing and reach a result that most of the testers are satisfied with the design.

Solution

RooMate is a roommate-seeker app, which helps people find, match, and communicate with future roommates. People can either post on RooMate or search othersâ€™ posts to find the ideal roommate. At the same, RooMate is also matching roommates based on peopleâ€™s preference automatically. The goal is to streamline the roommate matching process and make it easier, safer and more fun.

Discovery

Before diving into user research, there are some hypotheses that can be validated by the later research result:

- It is not easy to find an ideal roommate and people tend to seek help from others.
- People tend to trust whom they feel reliable and touchable.
- The more information (including photos, videos) provided, the more reliable and trustful people will feel towards others.
- People wonâ€™t feel safe if their contact information is disclosed publicly, especially phone numbers.
- From privacy purpose, people do not want to share their social media account

User Research is conducted to further refine the problem statement, validate the assumptions listed above, dig into the unique needs of people whom Iâ€™m designing for, understand their pain point and define the audience. Research findings become a touchstone for ensuring that creative and aesthetic decisions are being made objectively later .In this project, I conducted the user survey and user interview.

User Interview

Interviews are conducted afterwards to help further explore the responses from the survey. I interviewed people who can represent different groups which are identified in the surveys. The following empathy map shows how one of the interviewees, BB, thinks, feels, says and does.

Main Takeaways:
- Users can be further separated into 3 groups: people who is renting out place and seeking roommates, but is not the owner of the property; people who is renting out place and seeking roommates, and owns the property; people who is renting a place and seeking roommates
- The 3 groups all care about some common habits of roommates when living together but at different tolerance level
- Users donâ€™t use roommate-finder app because they have never heard of such apps
- Users are not reluctant to link their social accounts as long as they have control of the access level

Personas

User research provides a good basis for creating the user personas. There are 3 distinct groups of people identified in user research thus 3 personas are created here: Mike Josh, Chris Chung and Dan Ivanov.

Mike Josh

Age: 33
Profession: Audit Manager
Region: Seattle

Bio

Mike is an Audit Manager working in Seattle. He currently is renting a 2-bedroom apartment, and look for another roommate to share the rent. Mike travels a lot due to his job and sometimes he will be on business trip for 3 weeks a month.

Motivation

Mike wants to share the rent with roommate to decrease the financial pressure. He is looking for a website or app that can match him with a perfect roommate in an easy way.

Frustrations

1. Difficult to find a good roommate
2, Hard to know more details of the potential roommate, e.g. their lifestyle
3, Trust issue
4, The rent is expensive
5, Look for a roommate who does not mind if he travels a lot

Chris Chung

Age: 24
Profession: Student
Region: New York

Bio

Chris Chung is a student in New York University who will graduate this summer. She is currently living in dorm provided by university but she needs to move out and find another place after she graduated. Since the rent in NY is pretty expensive, Chris decides to find a roommate to share the rent.

Motivation

Chris is looking for a place that is cost-bearable, with convenient transporation options, e.g. close to subway station, bus stop, and living friendly, e.g. close to grocery stores. She is looking for a roommate around her age with similar education background.

Frustrations

1. Expensive to rent place in New York City, especially in Manhattan
2, Does not like roommate who has alcohol or drug issue
3, Allergic to dogs
4, Look for a roommate who does not mind if she parties a lot

Dan Brown

Age: 31
Profession: Software Engineer
Region: Mountain View

Bio

Dan Brown is living in Mountain View and working at Google as a software engineer. He bought a townhouse near Google campus, and looking for 2 roommates who can rent his spare room. He has a cute dog living with him.

Motivation

Dan prefers the roommates working in tech, especially working at Google, thus he can have more topics with roommates. He likes to play video games in his spare time, so he is looking for roommates who also likes video games.

Frustrations

Comparative Analysis

To see where RooMate stands in the roommate-seeking and house-sharing market, I selected 4 competitors, which are Roomi, RoomEasy, Airbnb and Craigslist. Among these competitors, Roomi and RoomEasy are direct competitors who have similar product offerings, and Airbnb and Craigslist are indirect competitors who have certain sections serve the similar purpose.

Main Takeaways:
- There is a huge demand in the market for the roommate-seeking app among strangers other than family and friends. The popularity of Craigslist and Airbnb can reflect this demand
- People are expecting their privacy to be protected. The popularity of in-app chat reflects this trend.
- Insights on roommate matching method: manual matching by user and automatic matching by system.

Visual Design

Look For A Room

People who are looking for a place can go to the Search Room Tab and browse room information posted by others. System will prioritize the matching posts and put it to front. People can also set the filter by location, price, roomtype, rules, roommateâ€™s gender etc. Once they find some interesting post, they can read more details by clicking the post and contact the host if they want to learn more.

Look For A Roommate

People who are looking for a roommate can go to Search Roommate Tab and search or browse information posted by others. Posts which are matching their preference are put in front and people can also set filters on gender, location, age etc. Once they are interested in the post, they can contact the people who post.

Post

No matter whether you want to rent out your spare room or look for a place to rent, you can post on RooMate. There are 2 options to post: Rent vs Rent Out.

Chat Within RooMate

People can choose to chat with others without revealing their actual contact information. By just clicking the chat button or go to the Chat Tab, a conversation can take place!

Onboarding

Users can sign up via Facebook, Google, and Apple account. They are also able to create a new account using email address. Once they log into the app, they can have a profile page that they can do the settings about themselves and preference towards future roommate, which can help our system quickly match the room or roommate and put the related posts to front when they browse post.

Final Thoughts

If given more time, what I will do ...

When doing the user research, I found out that people are very concerned about the background check of potential roommates. The trust issue, if not handled well, might destroy all the good user experience. So if I had more time on this project, I would focus more on how to make people feel secure and comfortable on RooMate. For example, it can require users to submit more identity related information when they are posting. Another way that can improve people's trust level in future is keeping out the spam or fake post. It can be done by verifying the authenticity of the address, and adding more interactions between users, e.g. exploring online open house tours etc.

What I have learned from this project

- Design process is not a linear process, but full of iterations
- Design is â€˜not about youâ€™, but about business and users
- Love testing! Always test the design and revise based on the results
- Be research-informed (as much as possible)
