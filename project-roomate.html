Challenge

There are a few trends of note: 54% of the world’s population live in urban communities, the average marriage age for men is 29 (up from 26 two decades ago) and for women is 27 (up from 23 in the same time period). Given these trends, city dwellers tend to spend most of their twenties living with roommates. Finding and keeping a good roommate, however, gets harder as more and more people live in cities.

Solution

RooMate is a roommate-seeker app, which helps people find, match, and communicate with future roommates. People can either post on RooMate or search others’ posts to find the ideal roommate. At the same, RooMate is also matching roommates basing on people’s preference automatically. The goal is to streamline the roommate matching process and make it easier, faster and more fun.

User Survey

User survey is conducted as the first step in User Research. Surveys are sent out via Google Forms. Keys questions are listed as below:

How do people find roommates?
What app or software do they use?
What factors do they consider when looking for roommates?
What make an ideal roommate to people?
What information do they want to reveal to public?

Here are some key insights from survey:

  • 91%

    have experience living with roommate

  • 60%

    find roommates via family or friends

  • 0%

    used roommate-finder app

  • 75%

    are concerned with alcohol issue of roommate

  • Top 3

    factors when people looking for a place are: price, location and quality of room

  • Top 4

    factors that people will consider when choosing roommates are: personality, similar lifestyle, age and gender

Personas

User surveys and interviews above provide a good basis for creating the user personas. There are 3 distinct groups of people identified in user research thus 3 personas are created here: Mike Josh, Chris Chung and Dan Ivanov.

Mike Josh

Age: 33
Profession: Audit Manager
Region: Seattle

Bio

Mike is an Audit Manager working in Seattle. He currently is renting a 2-bedroom apartment, and look for another roommate to share the rent. Mike travels a lot due to the job and sometimes he will be on business trip for 3 weeks a month.

Motivation

Mike wants to share the rent with roommate to decrease the financial pressure. He is looking for a website or app that can match him with a perfect roommate in an easy way.

Frustration

1. Difficult to find a good roommate
2, Hard to know more details of the potential roommate, e.g. their lifestyle
3, Trust issue
4, The rent is expensive
5, Look for a roommate who does not mind if he travels a lot

Chris Chung

Age: 24
Profession: Student
Region: New York

Bio

Chris Chung is a student in New York University who will graduate this summer. She is currently living in dorm provided by university but she needs to move out and find another place after she graduated. Since the rent in NY is pretty expensive, Chris decides to find a roommate to share the rent.

Motivation

Chris is looking for a place that is cost-bearable, with convenient transporation options, e.g. close to subway station, bus stop, and living friendly, e.g. close to grocery stores. She is looking for a roommate around her age with similar education background.

Frustration

1. Expensive to rent place in New York City, especially in Manhattan
2, Does not like roommate who has alcohol or drug issue
3, Allergic to dogs
4, Look for a roommate who does not mind if she parties a lot

Comparative Analysis

To see where RooMate stands in the roommate-finding and house renting market, I selected 3 competitors, which are Roomi, RoomEasy and Airbnb.

Roomi and RoomEasy are focusing on helping people find roommates for long terms either by algorithm or manually. However, their brand awareness is very low per the suvery results showed and it leaves a big market for RooMate to explore and also insights on future marketing promotion strategy.

Airbnb, on the other hand, starts its ideas from sharing the spare room with strangers, mostly short term. However, now Airbnb is sort of deviating from its original model and focusing more on creating different and better (even luxurious) experiences to travelers and not surprisingly, travelers are somewhat spoiled. They are expecting more service and better quality from Airbnb, and some of them no longer want to share room with others.

Usability Testing on Wireframes

A guerrilla usability testing is conducted to test the low-fi wireframe on Maze. There are 4 tasks that testers are asked to complete under instructions. Tasks are listed below:

1. Sign up via Google account
2. Browse the housing post and contact the host
3. Edit and post to rent out spare room
4. Browse potential roommate and chat

Here are key improvement from the usability testing results:

Sign Up

Differentiate the Google account button as well as other website account by choosing different color.

Roommate Search

Since one of the testers gave up in Task 4 by missing the roommate tab, I will consider to make it the same obvious to user as the housing tab by using similar icons, text and layout.

Visual Design

Onboarding

Users can sign up via Facebook, Google, and Apple account. They are also able to create a new account using email address. Once they log into the app, they can have a profile page that they can do the settings about themselves and preference towards future roommate, which can help our system quickly match the room or roommate and put the related posts to front when they browse post.

Look For A Room

People who are looking for a place can go to the Search Room Tab and browse room information posted by others. System will prioritize the matching posts and put it to front. People can also set the filter by location, price, roomtype, rules, roommate’s gender etc. Once they find some interesting post, they can read more details by clicking the post and contact the host if they want to learn more.

Look For A Roommate

People who are looking for a roommate can go to Search Roommate Tab and search or browse information posted by others. Posts which are matching their preference are put in front and people can also set filters on gender, location, age etc. Once they are interested in the post, they can contact the people who post.

Post

No matter whether you want to rent out your spare room or look for a place to rent, you can post on RooMate. There are 2 options to post: Rent vs Rent Out.

Chat Within RooMate

People can choose to chat with others without revealing their actual contact information. By just clicking the chat button or go to the Chat Tab, a conversation can take place!

Final Thoughts

When doing the user research, I found out that people are actually very concerned about the background check of potential roommate. The trust issue, if not handled well, might destroy all the good user experience. So if I had more time on this project, I would focus more on how to make people feel secured and comfortable on RooMate. For example, it can require users to submit more identity related information when they are posting. Another point that can improve in future is keeping out the spam or fake post. It can be done by verifying the authenticity of the address, and adding more interactions between users, e.g. exploring online open house tour etc.
