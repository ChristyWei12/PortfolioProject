What is WeCloud?

WeCloud is a secured online platform where you can save your files and share with others. It aims to bring a fun and pleasant experience for busy professions, especially in tech, and students in college and university.

Problem

Nowadays the cloud storage and organization product is becoming more and more popular, however, most of the existing products are too complicated - users often find it hard to organize the files due to complexity of the structure, or lacking fun to use - the UI is looking too serious and formal.

Solution

WeCloud wants to blow a new fresh air into this field providing a more colorful, relaxing and fun user experience that users can upload files, save files and collaborate with others especially under the current trend of working and collaborating remotely.

Discovery

User Survey

User survey is helping me better understand on how people think about the cloud management market and what is their real desire. Surveys are sent out via Google Forms. Keys questions are listed as below:

What cloud management product do they use?
What features do they use most?
What are the deterrents that make them look for another app/website?
How do they like to share the file?
Are they using these kind of product for personal or business use?

Here are some key insights from survey:

  • 89%
    of attendees have experience using cloud content management website/app
  • 96%
    of attendees use the website/app to upload files to the cloud
  • 76%
    of attendees will stop using the product if there is no free space or very limited space provided
  • 64%
    of attendees would like to share the files via link

Personas

User survey provides a good basis for creating the user personas. 2 distinct groups are identified among survey attendees:

the student group who are studying in university or graduate school and have the need to save files and share with others for academic projects;

the business professionals who are working in a company and have needs to process work files and also collaborate with others at work.

Comparative Analysis

The competition in the cloud content management market is very fierce and many big brands are stepping into this field. I did a competitive Analysis of Google Drive, Dropbox and Egnyte, which are the big players in the market and got insights in Visual Design, User Flow, Brand & Logo design etc.

Usability Testing on Wireframes

Usability testing is conducted to test the low-fi wireframe either in person or over Zoom. There are 4 tasks in total that testers are asked to complete under instructions. Tasks are listed below:

1. Sign up
2. Upload pictures from their mobile device to the cloud space
3. Record an audio and save at the cloud
4. Organize files

Overall, all 3 participants can perform the tasks without any external help and they said it was not hard.

Below is the Affinity Map for analyzing the feedback:

Here are key improvements from the usability testing:

1, The Sign up process should keep shorter and allows users freedom to skip certain part
2, Use standard Google login page
3, Allow users to drag file to different folders
4, Add 'Create a new folder' under existing folders

Visual Design

Onboarding

There are 2 types of accounts on WeCloud: Personal and Business Account. WeCloud users can sign in via Google account and also sign in by entering registered email address.

Upload Files

People can upload files in different ways: uploading picture/videos from local album, taking a picture/video, recording audio, uploading text files, scanning letters or text and converting to text file etc.

Share Files

Files on WeCloud can be shared with others via email invitation, shareable link, QR code, or zip folder. While sharing files, people can manage the access level of whether others can comment, edit or just view. Sharing files are protected by password.

Organize Files

Files are organized under different folders. It is very easy for users to drag or change the location of files. People can also set the favorable file with star so it will be listed in the star folder as well.

Final Thoughts

This is my first project when I began to study UX design. As a solo project, I would like to say thanks to my mentor as well as the grading team for supporting me a lot. This project enables me to work, think and talk as a real designer. I am excited to see its growth from nothing to a refined product. Due to the limited time and budget, there is still a lot of space that WeCloud can improve. For example, it is still very similar to Google Drive and Dropbox in many functions. To stand out among the competitions, WeCloud needs to figure out its uniqueness.

If given more time on this project, I would like to focus on developing the social collaboration feature of WeCloud. For example, I would like to create common groups or common rooms (or we can call it common cubes) for teams. So teams can create a virtual space on WeCloud so team members can get access from their WeCloud dashboard with different access. It is like Slack chat channel but more like a folder space.
